How we would think about the stack

Most of the work is not AI. Speaker drivers, firmware, the companion app, the inventory and fulfillment network, the tariff-diversification play: that is traditional code, hardware, and operations. Rule-based logic covers the voice-command tree, the Siri-Shortcut layer, the preset phrases, the pairing flow. The genuine AI layer is smaller than the marketing suggests and more interesting than the current implementation allows. It is the place where a safety-frame user logs an incident without pulling out a phone, where a technician on a shift gets a voice-native checklist tied to the job, where a retail optician gets a spoken summary of the customer's order history while they fit the frames. That is the orchestration layer. It sits above the hardware and below the enterprise buyer's workflow. Build it small, build it legible, and the "AI-enabled" claim stops being marketing and starts being a line item a compliance officer can sign off on.
